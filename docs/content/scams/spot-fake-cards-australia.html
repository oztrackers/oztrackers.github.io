How to Spot Fake Pokémon and TCG Cards in Australia

Key checks to avoid counterfeit cards when buying online or in person in Australia

Visual and print checks

Compare suspected cards to verified originals - look for incorrect colours, fuzzy printing, or off-centre borders. Counterfeits often have muted or oversaturated tones compared to official prints.

Weight and card stock

Authentic cards use specific card stock. If you can, compare weight and stiffness to a known genuine card. Counterfeits can feel flimsier or bend differently.

Holograms and security marks

Many legitimate cards include foil patterns, holographic stamps, or set-specific security marks. Research known markers for the set you are buying and inspect under good lighting.

Seller reputation and photos

Ask sellers for detailed photos of the card edges, back, and front. Request scans if possible. Poor-quality or low-resolution images are a red flag.

When in doubt

Seek a second opinion from reputable grading services or experienced local retailers. For high-value purchases consider professional authentication.
