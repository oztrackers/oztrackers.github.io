Buying Singles in Australia - Online vs In-store Pros and Cons

Compare the benefits and tradeoffs of purchasing single cards online or in person, with actionable tips for Australian buyers.

Online buying - advantages

  • Wide selection and ability to compare prices quickly.
  • Access to international sellers and rare listings.
  • Buyer protection on platforms like eBay Australia.

Online buying - disadvantages

  • Condition may be misrepresented in photos.
  • Shipping costs and wait times add to total cost.
  • Scams and fake listings are possible on open marketplaces.

In-store buying - advantages

  • Inspect condition in person and avoid misleading photos.
  • Immediate pickup - no shipping wait.
  • Support local shops and build relationships with sellers.

In-store buying - disadvantages

  • Limited selection compared to online marketplaces.
  • Prices may be higher for rare singles due to consignment fees.
  • Regional availability varies outside major cities.

How to safely buy singles online in Australia

  1. Check seller feedback and read all photos carefully.
  2. Ask for additional photos of front, back, and edges.
  3. Use tracked postage and insure high-value cards.
  4. Consider grading services for expensive singles - see PSA.

Where to look

  • eBay Australia - auctions and buy-it-now listings
  • Local game stores running singles boxes or consignment
  • Facebook Marketplace and Gumtree for local pickup, but exercise caution

Whether you buy online or in person, use the checks above to protect your purchase and confirm condition before completing a transaction.
